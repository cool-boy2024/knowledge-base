# Research Workflows

Patterns and practices for research work in this vault.

---

## Typical Research Session

**1. Daily Work**: In `Daily/YYYY-MM-DD.md`, link to concepts being worked on, papers read, external resources found. Use wikilinks throughout.

**2. Process External Content**: Highlights → vault notes. Blog posts → external-resource notes. Papers → literature notes. Use `/encode` for all note creation.

**3. Link Concepts to Implementation**: Concept notes reference repo work. Daily notes link both. Project note tracks overall progress.

**4. Commit & Sync**: Commit after changes. The knowledge graph rebuilds from frontmatter, keeping SPARQL queries current.

---

## Research Session Skill

Use `/research-session` for structured deep dives. It provides 5 cognitive modes:

1. **Explore** — cast wide net, follow connections
2. **Architect** — organize findings, identify structure
3. **Synthesize** — build arguments from 3+ sources
4. **Critique** — stress-test, find gaps
5. **Ship** — commit, update MOCs, retro

Each mode has entry gates preventing premature transitions (e.g., can't synthesize without 3+ source notes).

---

## Project-Research Integration

Production projects can bridge research themes. Document connections in a "Research Connections" section of project notes, linking to concept notes in `03 - Resources/`.

**Pattern**: `01 - Projects/[Project].md` → "Research Connections" → `[[03 - Resources/Concept]]`

**See**: `/project-planning` skill for full project methodology

---

## Terminology Precision

When working in specialized domains, maintain precise terminology distinctions. Add domain-specific precision notes to your area MOCs as your vault vocabulary develops.

---

## Cross-Area Research Connections

Research isn't isolated — it connects to other areas of focus:
- Learning new tools and methods serves Self-Development
- Health and cognitive practices shape research workflow design
- Collaborations bridge Family & Relationships and Research

When research work touches another area, link to both the research MOC and the relevant area note.
